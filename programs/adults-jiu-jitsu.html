The Traditional Gi

Training in the Gi (the traditional kimono) is a highly technical, strategic game. Because grips can be established on the collar, sleeves, and pants, the pace is often more methodical, requiring deep problem-solving and leverage.

Gi training is incredible for developing defensive layers and technical precision, as you must learn to navigate and break strong grips to advance your position.

  • Highly Technical Grip Fighting
  • Collar & Sleeve Chokes

Modern No-Gi

Shed the kimono and step into the fast-paced world of No-Gi submission grappling. Wearing just a rashguard and shorts, you can no longer rely on grabbing fabric to control your opponent.

Instead, No-Gi relies on wrestling ties, underhooks, and body-lock control. The game is faster, more slippery, and incorporates modern leg-locking systems heavily utilized in MMA and submission-only competitions.

  • Fast-Paced & Wrestling Heavy
  • Modern Leg Lock Systems
